Catch the Giving Bug

About Us

Our Story

Once upon a time there were many giving bugs. (A giving bug is a bug that never stops giving hope and joy to everyone.) However, people stopped catching them and giving them to those in need, so the giving bugs felt sad and hid away. Help us catch the giving bugs, and give them to people in need.

Our Purpose

Our purpose is to raise money for different foundations by creating a website that sells handmade products. The money raised will then be donated to these foundations.

Founders

We are three sisters from the Bay Area in California. We started out selling cookies at bake sales and helping out at soup kitchens. Now that we are older, we want to leverage the internet to help others in need. All proceeds from product sales will be donated to charity. Every product is handmade by us from our own home. We hope that by leveraging the internet, more people can help support our cause. Please help support our cause by buying our products and by sending out email chain letters to your friends. Together, we can all make a difference!

Lava Cake Sales


Much of our funds have come from lava cake and brownie sales held in school. These delicious and savory desserts are popular among students and teachers and quickly sell out every time. So far, we have raised over $400 from these food sales alone.

x

"I Helped End Hunger" Button Sales


This fundraiser not only raises money for Plan USA, but also promotes awareness of the hunger that exists in many poverty-striken societies.

x

Friendship Bracelet Sales


This fundraiser came about from the friendship-bracelet trend at our high school campus, helping us raise over $300!

x

Snowman Soup Sales


In the spirit of the holiday season, we sold packages of snowman soup, which consisted of: hot chocolate powder, mini marshmallows, crushed peppermints, and a stirring stick. This festive fundraiser was a sure hit, helping us raise over $200!

x

Poverty Awareness Week


Over the course of a week, the Catch the Giving Bug Club at Aragon High School raised awareness about poverty through various activities. The club distributed pledge cards to students on how they can help end poverty; these cards were displayed for the school to see. The club also sold friendship bracelets to raise money, held a Wear White Day for students to show their support against poverty, made daily announcements about how students can end poverty, and postered the school walls with facts about poverty!

x

Glide Soup Kitchen


Around Thanksgiving time, we took our club from school to the Glide Soup Kitchen in San Francisco. We were able to make over 700 sandwiches in our few hours there! It was an incredible and worthwhile experience!
